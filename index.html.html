Our Story

A History of Faith & Trade

Founded in 1578, Agoo is the oldest town in La Union. Once known as "Puerto de Japon," it was a bustling trading center long before the Spanish arrived. Its name comes from the "Aroo" or pine-like trees that line the riverbanks.

Mission

To preserve Agoo's heritage while building a world-class sustainable tourism environment.

Vision

To become the heart of cultural and eco-tourism in Northern Luzon by 2030.

Agoo, La Union, Philippines
Basilica Minore

Located in the town proper, this basilica is home to Nuestra Señora de Caridad. It features a unique mix of Mexican and Baroque architecture and serves as a major pilgrimage site in Northern Luzon.

Agoo Eco Park

The Agoo Eco Park features breathtaking views of the coastline, man-made lagoons, and lush mangrove forests. It is an ideal spot for eco-tourism, family picnics, and jogging.

Agoo Beach

Agoo Beach offers a serene escape with its wide grey sand shores. It is famous for its stunning golden hour views and is a favorite local destination for weekend swimming.

Eagle of the North

This massive concrete monument of a spreading eagle marks the gateway to the Ilocos Region. It stands as a symbol of the courage and soaring spirit of the people of the North.

Museo Iloko

Museo Iloko preserves the cultural heritage of Agoo and the Ilocano people. It houses antique religious icons, traditional agricultural tools, and artifacts dating back centuries.
